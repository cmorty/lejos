Circle Navigation – driving in the arc of a circle.

Roger Glassey

 

Problem: how to control the robot (with independently controlled drive wheels)  to move in a circle of specified radius and then update the robot position.

 

Geometry:

As the robot moves in a circle, the wheels trace concentric circles.

The speeds of the two wheels can be independently controlled.  The dimensions of the robot and the speed ratio determine the robot path

Definitions:

w = wheel spacing. (AKA track width)

d = wheel diameter

ro = radius of outside wheel trace.

ri =  radius of outside wheel trace = ro -w

ρ = speed ratio (inside wheel speed / outside wheel speed) – the control variable

ρ = ri/ ro

φ = robot angle of rotation

θo = revolution angle of outside wheel

θi = revolution angle of inside wheel

 

Steering control -

 

Assuming the wheel rolls around the circular arc without slipping, we have

eq 1     φ ro = θo d/2

eq 2     φ ri = θi  d/2

eq 3     φ (ro –w) = θi  d/2

Subtracting eq 3 from eq 1 gives

eq 4     φ w = (θo - θi) d/2     or

eq 5     φ w = θo (1 – ρ) d/2. 

This relates the robot rotation angle to the outside wheel revolution angle, as determined by the control variable. In particular,

eq 6     θo = 2 φ w / (1 – ρ) d  determines the wheel rotation angle required  to produce a target a robot rotation angle

Navigation – updating the robot position after traveling an arc:

In the robot coordinate system, the robot is at the origin, heading in the positive x direction.

In this coordinate system, the center of the arc is at (0,r)

After traversing the arc, the robot  position  R΄ is at coordinates dx, dy.

eq 12   dx = r sin(φ)

eq 13   dy = r (1 – cos(φ) )

The polar coordinate representation of the new robot position are given by  z, ψ. where ψ is the angle the displacement vector makes the angle to the robot x axis.

eq 14   ψ =  arctan(dy/dx). 

The length of the displacement vector z is given by

eq 15   z = sqrt(dx2 + dy2 )

Let x0 , y0 be the original location of the robot in the world coordinate system, and α.  be the robot direction angle.  Then the displacement vector makes an angle α.+ ψ  to the world x axis.    Converting this vector to rectangular world coordinated, the new robot position is calculated from:

eq 16   x = x0 + z cos(α.+ ψ  )  and

eq 17   y = y0 +  z sin(α.+ ψ)  

The new robot direction angle is α + φ.
